04. How to change the sky and layer two images together

  • Open Photoshop: Launch Adobe Photoshop on your computer.
  • Open the Images: Open both the image of the sky with clouds and the image of the girl looking back in Photoshop.
  • Select and Copy the Sky Image: Use any selection tool (like the Lasso Tool or Quick Selection Tool) to select the sky with clouds in the first image. Once selected, copy the sky image by pressing Ctrl + C (Command + C on Mac).
  • Paste the Sky Image: Go to the image with the girl, and paste the copied sky image on top of it by pressing Ctrl + V (Command + V on Mac). Photoshop will create a new layer with the pasted sky image above the girl's image.
  • Position and Resize the Sky Image: Use the Move Tool (V) to position the pasted sky image where you want it to appear in the girl's image. You can resize the sky image if needed by selecting Edit > Free Transform (Ctrl + T / Command + T) and dragging the handles.
  • Blend the Sky Image: To blend the new sky image with the girl's image, adjust the layer blending mode and opacity in the Layers panel. Experiment with different blending modes like Overlay, Soft Light, or Screen to achieve a natural blend between the sky and the girl's image.
  • Add the Planet: Open the image containing the planet in Photoshop. Use selection tools to select the circular area around the planet. Copy the selected area by pressing Ctrl + C (Command + C on Mac).
  • Paste the Planet: Go back to the image with the girl and paste the copied planet on top of the sky layer by pressing Ctrl + V (Command + V on Mac). Position the planet where you want it to appear in the sky.
  • Blend the Planet: Use the Eraser Tool or layer masks to blend the edges of the planet seamlessly into the sky. Adjust the opacity and blending mode of the planet layer to make it look more natural in the sky.
  • Final Touches: Once you're satisfied with the composition, make any final adjustments to color, lighting, or contrast to enhance the overall image.
